I made this decision saddened, and frankly angered, at the outpouring of hatred and harassment towards myself and my supporters, including a minor, who received a threatening phone call. 

I know that the recent announcement by Scott Hall, another candidate for District 90 School Board, that he was suspending his campaign due to harassment and intimidation, triggered an emotional response. As I said in my previous statement regarding his campaign suspension, harassment and intimidation have absolutely no place in a free and fair democracy, nor in the electoral or campaign processes. However, harnessing those emotions to harass and intimidate another candidate is not the right action to take.

Unfortunately, I can sympathize even more with Scott, now that I have experienced the harassment and intimidation he mentioned in his announcement.

Sarah's statement on Scott Hall suspending his campaign
Thursday, March 24

I was saddened to hear today that Scott Hall suspended his campaign for District 90 School Board. While Scott and I did enter the race for School Board with different goals in mind, it is disappointing that his campaign has ended early, and that his supporters will no longer be able to support their candidate.

In his campaign suspension announcement, Mr. Hall claimed “I, my family, my friends and even local businesses with which I associate have been repeatedly threatened, harassed and disparaged.” I want to be clear that threats, harassment, and disparagement have no place in a campaign, nor in a fair and free democracy.

While I don’t doubt Mr. Hall’s claims, I want to ensure that his parting words do not cast our campaign in a negative light, and I want to provide everyone in the community with a more full series of events. I am sharing a letter, and the subsequent correspondence, which was recently sent to the superintendent of District 90, Ed Condon, and the District 90 School Board:
